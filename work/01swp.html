SE15 for Maria Gorodekaya (2019)

A manifestation of a cycle in three events around August’s Moon cycle. Combining sculpture, music and performance, in SE15 Gorodeckaya continues an investigation into the power dynamics within which identities are determined through both affective and affected bodies.
For one of the cycles, a choreographic structure was deviced in collaboration with 21 male-bodied performers residents in London. The performance took place at Jupiter Woods Gallery, Southeast London.

Concient of the Soul for Cõvco (2019)

A performance by artist and musician Cõvco explores the mortality of a woman through rituals addressing limitations of the mind and body and the fear of facing reality.
Commissioned to collaboratively device the choreography and perform it at South London Gallery, as part of the photographer Liz Jhonson Arthur’s exhibition ‘If you know the beginning, the end is no trouble’ in Peckham, London.

Modern Couples Intervention for Barbican Centre (2018-2019)
In collaboration with Monika Błaszczak

A series of living sculptures that intervened the Barbican’s main gallery space, manifesting the partnered creative phenomenon that birthed the intention of the exhibition, offering the living artistic body as canvases of presence in space. This performance took place alongside original art pieces by Picasso and Maar, Dalí and García Lorca, Khalo and Rivera, Klimt and Flöge, among many others.

From Mjøsa to the sea of Okhotsk (2018)
In collaboration with Jana Winderen, Mia Winderen and Jon Werede Hope and Lena Winderen

Using sound recordings of the Taiga Forest from around the world, Jana Winderen presented a soundscape in her farmland in Mjøsa, Norway, as part of the Festival Mjøsa - et Kunstprosjekt, in a collective effort to bring awareness to ecological threats imposed by urban development. The performance was a choreographic devise that welcomed visitors to experience the sound installation, aside from recreating the spirit of the lake utilising materials that were intrinsic to Norwegian farm-work.

Commissioned work for Anna Talvi's microgravity garment design (2018)

Collaboration for the presentation of Talvi’s MA project at Royal College of Art in London with photographer Benedict Redgrove., devising choreography and performing with garments designed to target changes that happen to the human physiology and psychology when in weightless environments.
